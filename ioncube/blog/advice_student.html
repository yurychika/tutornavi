For Students

TutorNavi helps language tutors and students connect with each other. Native speakers who would like to teach language may register as tutors. Students may select their tutors by reading the tutors’ blogs and comments from other students. Since TutorNavi does not hire registered tutors, we cannot guarantee the tutors’ teaching ability. Please take time to read the following tips when you register as a tutor.

  • (1)
    Students are not permitted to give out their personal contact information (such as telephone number, email address, homepages, links and etc ) on students’ profile page. If this happens, their profile will be deleted.
  • (2)
    Register--Search for a Student--(Apply for TutorNavi Membership)--Communicate--Start Teaching. Each tutor must go through these steps in order to start a lesson.
  • (3)
    TutorNavi is an introduction service. For all other enquiries or requests, please feel free to contact our office.
  • (4)
    Students are not permitted to introduce their tutor to someone else who is not registered with TutorNavi.
  • (5)
    Students should deal directly with their tutor in regards to the tuition fee and payment method.
    We suggest the student pay the tutor at the end of each lesson. Please make sure what the hourly tuition fee is and the fee’s effective date with the tutor using our internal messaging system before the lesson, because some tutors may increase their fee without updating their latest information.TutorNavi does not resolve disputes between tutors and the students about the raising tuition.
  • (6)
    A tutor can’t always be judged by their appearance.
    TutorNavi provides information on the tutors to help students find the best tutor. Students may learn more about the tutor by reading his/her blogs and the comments, as well as visiting the student/tutor community. Check these points before choosing a tutor. Not all native speakers are qualified to be great language tutors.
